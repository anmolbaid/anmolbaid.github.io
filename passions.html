Writing
Writing is about painting pictures in the mind but using words in black and white. I write poetry, essays, articles and rants. My writing is usually the reflection of my own thoughts. Across my social media, between the pages of my diary, on the backside of bills and in the stack of single pages on my shelf, my pencil has scribbled words everywhere.
Travelling
There is no place in the world, where I wouldn't gaga over a sunset. I've travelled to France, Italy, Switzerland, Dubai, Vatican City, Rajasthan and few other cities in the world. I dig understanding how cultures and societies change with longitudinal variations, but somehow I feel, people are the same everywhere. Paris is so far, my favourite spot in the world.
Archery
The sport of focus and precision, archery, is my key to channelizing my attentive energy towards bull's eye. I am an intermediate level at modern style target archery with a recurve bow.
Equestrianism
Also known as horseriding, this is among my go-to thrills on a weekend. The experience of taming a horse, from getting the horse to understand your gestures to progressing from a trot to a gallop is very enriching. I am an intermediate rider.
Cooking
My favourite ingredient is cocoa. Baking is my sinful self-indulging leisure that leaves me tempted. I am a staunch believer in the religion of desserts and chocolate making is my afternoon art. My brownies, puddings, choco-chip muffins, shakes and nutty dark chocolates are an oversold show.
Painting
I like to doodle and splash colours that create a thought in the viewer's mind. Storytelling, communicating an emotion or shifting perspective using colours or silhouette sketches leaves me satisfied. I have a level 2 degree (Chitra Bikash) in painting and sketching.
Reading
I wouldn't want more in life if I could find a cosy swing chair, a hot chocolate mug and all the books in the world. I read both fiction and non-fiction. A classic piece of literature from Jane Austen, murder-mystery-thriller-crimes from Sidney Sheldon or Dan Brown, war strategies from Sun Tzu or Robert Greene.
